Step 0

Start from factory defaults

Important: Resetting deletes current custom settings and banks. Skip reset if the camera already contains settings you need. Photograph important menu screens first.

1. Check firmware

SETUP MENU → Firmware version

Use Nikon’s current Z8 firmware before building the banks. Menu labels can vary by firmware.

2. Reset photo settings

PHOTO SHOOTING MENU → Reset photo shooting menu

Choose Yes only when you are ready to rebuild from scratch.

3. Reset custom settings

CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → Reset custom settings

This restores custom controls and autofocus behavior to defaults.

4. Reset video settings

VIDEO RECORDING MENU → Reset video recording menu

Then switch the photo/video selector to video before configuring Video Banks.

Visual control guide

Buttons, dials and controls

These original simplified diagrams highlight where the important controls are located. They are not copied from Nikon’s manual.

Front controls

Focus-mode button: hold it and rotate the rear dial for AF-S/AF-C/MF; rotate the front dial for Wide C1, Wide S, 3D tracking or Single-point.

Fn1/Fn2: programmable shortcuts. This guide uses Fn1 for 3D-tracking override and Fn2 for Recall shooting functions.

Rear controls

AF-ON: starts autofocus when using back-button focus or can be customized for focus hold.

Joystick/sub-selector: moves the AF box and confirms the chosen subject.

i button: opens the fast menu where your bank, subject detection, AF area, metering and VR should live.

Protect/zoom: useful for 100% playback focus checks.

Top controls

MODE: choose Manual exposure.

Release: select burst rate or self-timer.

ISO: change ISO; hold ISO and rotate the front dial to toggle Auto ISO.

± EV: fine-tune exposure while Auto ISO is active.

🎨 Ejaz Khan–inspired composition

Rule of thirds: place the eye near an intersection.

Negative space: leave room toward gaze or movement.

Symmetry: center only when shape and eye contact are strong.
