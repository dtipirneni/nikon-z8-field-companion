With **Slot 2 set to Overflow**, the Z8 records to the primary card until it is full and then continues on the second card. This maximizes total capacity but does not create an in-camera duplicate. Use the CFexpress Type B card in Slot 1 as the primary card and the SD card in Slot 2 for overflow. Back up the day's files to another device whenever possible.

## Build from factory defaults

1. Photograph any existing settings you may want to preserve.
2. Before resetting, use **SETUP MENU → Save/load menu settings → Save menu settings** to create a restorable card backup of the current setup.
3. Open **PHOTO SHOOTING MENU → Shooting menu bank**, highlight Bank A, press **O (Q)**, and confirm **Yes**. Repeat for B, C, and D. On the Z8, shooting banks are reset individually from the bank-selection screen.
4. Open **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → Custom settings bank**, highlight Bank A, press **O (Q)**, and confirm **Yes**. Repeat for B, C, and D.
5. Set **PHOTO SHOOTING MENU → Extended menu banks → ON**. This shared setting also enables extended exposure storage for video, while firmware 3.00 retains separate photo and video exposure values.
6. Build the Global, Photo, Video, and Custom sections in menu order.
7. Configure the i Menu, My Menu, paired banks, and controls.
8. Record a test photo and video in every bank and review the results.
9. Save the completed Tanzania configuration to a memory card using the procedure below.

## Save and restore the complete menu setup

Use the camera's built-in **Save/load menu settings** function after all four Photo, Video, and Custom Settings Banks have been configured and tested. This is separate from saving photographs: it creates a small settings file on a memory card.

### Save the finished Tanzania setup to a card

1. Insert a memory card with free space and make sure its write-protect switch, if present, is unlocked.
2. If both cards are inserted, confirm **PHOTO SHOOTING MENU → Primary slot selection**. The Z8 saves the settings file to the card selected as the primary slot. For this companion, that should normally be the **512 GB CFexpress Type B card in Slot 1**.
3. Press **MENU**.
4. Open the **SETUP MENU**—the wrench icon.
5. Select **Save/load menu settings**.
6. Select **Save menu settings** and confirm when prompted.
7. Wait for the camera to finish before turning it off or removing the card.
8. Turn the camera off, remove the selected card, and use a card reader to copy the camera-created **`NCSET***`** file to a clearly named computer folder, for example `Nikon Z8 Tanzania Settings - Final`.
9. Keep the original filename unchanged. The `***` identifier varies, and the Z8 cannot load the file if it is renamed.

### Load the Tanzania setup back into the camera

1. Before loading, save the camera's current setup first if it may be needed later; loading replaces the supported current menu settings.
2. Copy the original, unrenamed **`NCSET***`** file back onto a memory card. Keep it in the same top-level card location where the camera originally created it, not inside the DCIM picture folders.
3. Insert that card into the Z8. If two cards are inserted, the simplest method is to insert only the card containing the settings file until loading is complete.
4. Turn on the camera and press **MENU**.
5. Open **SETUP MENU → Save/load menu settings**.
6. Select **Load menu settings** and confirm when prompted. This choice appears only when the camera detects a compatible saved-settings file on an inserted card.
7. Wait for loading to finish, then turn the camera off and on again.
8. Verify Photo Banks A–D, Video Banks A–D, Custom Settings Banks A–D, the f1/g1 i Menus, f2/g2 custom controls, Slot 2 Overflow, My Menu, and the Fn2 recall values before an important shoot.
9. Confirm the current exposure mode, shutter speed, aperture, date/time, and lens-dependent VR choices. These should always be checked after a restore even when the related menu-bank features were saved.

### What the settings file includes—and what still needs checking

- It includes supported Photo and Video Recording Menu settings, **all Custom Settings**, My Menu, Recent Settings, and selected Playback and Setup Menu items. This covers the companion's banked autofocus behavior and f1/g1/f2/g2 control layouts.
- It is compatible only with another **Nikon Z8**, not a different Nikon model.
- Custom Picture Controls are saved as **Auto**, so separately preserve any personally created Picture Control files.
- The exact date and time, location position, timecode origin, and Network Menu connection settings are not part of the documented saved set.
- Vibration-reduction choices depend on the attached lens, so verify VR after mounting the 180–600mm or 70–180mm.
- The file is a settings backup, not a photo or video backup. Continue making separate daily copies of the image files.

Official Nikon reference: [Save/Load Menu Settings](https://onlinemanual.nikonimglib.com/z8/en/sum_save_load_menu_settings_346.html).

### Bank D shutter speed by focal length

The bank recalls only a starting shutter speed. It does **not** automatically change shutter speed as the lens is zoomed. In Manual exposure mode, rotate the **main command dial** whenever the focal length changes.

| Focal length | Starting shutter |
|---:|---:|
| 70mm | **5–7 sec; start at 5 sec** |
| 100mm | 4–5 sec |
| 135mm | 2–3 sec |
| 180mm | **1.6–2 sec** |

At 180mm, begin with **2 sec, f/2.8, ISO 6400**. Magnify the captured image and inspect the stars. If they appear elongated, shorten the shutter to 1.6 or 1.3 seconds. If the stars remain round but the image is too dark, raise ISO rather than lengthening the exposure.

For a low-light portrait rather than stars, temporarily use **AF-S or AF-C with People detection**, depending on whether the person is stationary or moving.

The 70–180mm f/2.8 is the preferred night lens because it admits substantially more light than the 180–600mm. Use 70mm when possible for longer star exposures and easier composition. Zooming toward 180mm requires much shorter exposures and higher ISO. The 180–600mm is not recommended for general night-sky work unless photographing the Moon or another bright, distant subject.

### Video and audio notes

- On the Z8, **H.265 10-bit MOV supports 3840×2160 60p**. Select the file type first, choose SDR, and then select the frame size/rate.
- Microphone sensitivity is gain, not a clipping-prevention switch. When using Manual, aim for ordinary peaks around −12 dB and loud peaks below −6 dB; never allow the meter to reach 0 dB.
- The attenuator reduces the incoming signal to protect against sudden loud sounds. Leave it Off for ordinary or quiet recording.
- With no external microphone, the Z8 uses its built-in stereo microphone. The empty mic jack and plug-in-power setting do not affect normal built-in-mic recording.
- Use a furry windscreen when practical. Electronic wind reduction cannot fully remove strong wind or open-vehicle turbulence.
- Hi-Res Zoom is disabled by default. With the 180–600mm, use optical zoom instead. In Bank C it may be enabled temporarily for a smooth digital push-in; set **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → g Video → g8 Hi-Res Zoom speed** to Slow or Medium.
- Hi-Res Zoom is compatible with Bank C at 4K 24/30p, but not with the A/B 4K 60/120p setups. While enabled, AF-area mode is fixed at **Wide-area AF (L)** and Electronic VR is forced **Off**; do not expect C’s AF-area cycling or walking e-VR to remain available.
- A normal full-resolution RAW photograph cannot be captured while actively recording video. Stop recording and switch to photo mode, or extract a JPEG frame later when the recording format supports it.

## Metering guide

| Metering mode | When to use it |
|---|---|
| **Matrix** | Default for all four banks. It evaluates most of the frame and is the most dependable general-purpose choice. |
| **Highlight-weighted** | Protects bright fur, feathers, clothing, clouds, and stage lighting from clipping. Particularly useful for white birds or pale animals in strong sun. |
| **Spot** | Use deliberately when one small subject area must control exposure, such as a face against a dramatically brighter or darker background. The reading can change sharply as the spot moves. |
| **Center-weighted** | Useful when the subject stays near the center and you want the middle of the frame to influence exposure more heavily. Keep the Custom Setting center-weighted area at Standard. |

In Banks A–C, metering still matters when using **Manual exposure with Auto ISO**: the camera changes ISO according to the meter. Apply exposure compensation when the result is consistently too bright or dark. In Bank D with Manual shutter, aperture, and ISO, metering does not change the exposure; use it only as a reference and confirm the result with the histogram and magnified test images.

### Show the live histogram while shooting

The live histogram must be added to a DISP screen separately for the rear monitor and electronic viewfinder. These display choices are stored in the active **Custom Settings Bank**, so repeat the setup in A, B, C, and D.

#### Photo mode: rear monitor

1. Select **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → Custom settings bank → A**.
2. Open **d Shooting/display → d19 Custom monitor shooting display**.
3. Make sure **Display 2** has a check mark. Highlight Display 2 and press **J** if it is unchecked.
4. Highlight **Display 2** and press **2/right** to choose its contents.
5. Check **Basic shooting info** and **Histogram**. Leave Grid or Virtual horizon unchecked on this particular display to keep the graph easy to read.
6. Press **MENU/G** to finish.
7. Repeat for Custom Settings Banks B, C, and D.
8. While composing a photograph on the rear monitor, press **DISP** repeatedly until the Display 2 screen with the live histogram appears.

#### Photo mode: electronic viewfinder

1. Open **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → d Shooting/display → d20 Custom viewfinder shooting display**.
2. Enable **Display 2**, highlight it, and press **2/right**.
3. Check **Basic shooting info** and **Histogram**, then press **MENU/G** to finish.
4. Repeat in Custom Settings Banks A–D.
5. Put your eye to the viewfinder and press **DISP** until the histogram display appears.

#### Video mode: monitor and viewfinder

1. Move the photo/video selector to video and select the appropriate Custom Settings Bank.
2. Open **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → g Video → g18 Brightness information display → Histogram**.
3. Press **2/right** on Histogram and set **Size: Standard**, **Transparency: 2**, and **Position: Bottom right**.
4. Open **g20 Custom monitor shooting display → Display 2**, enable it, press **2/right**, and check **Basic shooting info** plus **Brightness information**.
5. Open **g21 Custom viewfinder shooting display → Display 2** and check the same items.
6. Repeat in Custom Settings Banks A–D.
7. During video standby or recording, press **DISP** to cycle to the screen containing the histogram.

#### Reading it in the field

- The left side represents dark tones, the middle represents midtones, and the right side represents bright tones.
- A graph touching the right edge warns that bright fur, feathers, clouds, or reflections may be clipping. Reduce exposure compensation or exposure when those highlights must retain detail.
- A graph concentrated toward the left can be normal for night scenes. Do not brighten Bank D merely to center the histogram; protect stars and judge a captured test image.
- The live histogram is based on the camera preview and is an exposure guide, not an exact measurement of the full RAW file. Confirm critical photographs with the playback histogram and highlight warnings.
- Pressing the photo-mode Video record button assigned **Live view info display off** temporarily hides the histogram and other overlays; press it again to restore them.
- In Bank D, **Adjust for ease of viewing** can brighten the display independently of the final photograph. If **Starlight view (photo Lv)** is On, the Z8 does not display a live histogram at all. Use the playback histogram and a magnified captured test frame instead.

Official Nikon references: [The Monitor and DISP screens](https://onlinemanual.nikonimglib.com/z8/en/parts_of_monitor_11.html), [The Viewfinder and DISP screens](https://onlinemanual.nikonimglib.com/z8/en/parts_of_viewfinder_12.html), and [g20 Custom Monitor Shooting Display](https://onlinemanual.nikonimglib.com/z8/en/csmg_custom_monitor_shooting_display_289.html).

#### Fn2: “Recall shooting functions” versus “Recall shooting functions (hold)”

The Nikon names are counterintuitive:

| Nikon assignment | How it behaves | AF-ON available? | Recommended use |
|---|---|---:|---|
| **Recall shooting functions** | The recalled settings remain active only while Fn2 is physically held. Releasing Fn2 restores the bank settings. | **Yes** | **Recommended for this companion.** It is fast, temporary, and difficult to leave active accidentally. |
| **Recall shooting functions (hold)** | Press Fn2 once to latch the recalled settings; press it again to restore the previous settings. | **No** | Use only when a long sequence makes continuous button pressure uncomfortable and you are comfortable monitoring the recall icon. |

Assign the version **without “(hold)”**. It matches the emergency-action approach described by wildlife photographer Steve Perry: hold one control to obtain a second shooting configuration, then release it to return immediately to the original settings. Because this version can include AF-ON, Fn2 can change settings and start autofocus in one action.

#### Critical focus-mode limitation

Recall Shooting Functions does **not** change Focus mode. Keep A and B in AF-C. For the most predictable Fn2 behavior, keep C in AF-C as well; back-button focus still works for stationary subjects by releasing AF-ON after focus is acquired.

Bank D normally uses MF for stars. Fn2 therefore uses **Starlight view (photo Lv)**. If you temporarily need a handheld low-light action preset, reassign Fn2 to Recall Shooting Functions and change Focus mode to AF-C first; Recall Shooting Functions cannot change MF to AF-C.

#### Sub-selector center: ideal assignment and use

The sub-selector is the small joystick to the right of the viewfinder. Moving it repositions the focus point; pressing it straight inward activates **Sub-selector center**.

Use this exact assignment:

**CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → f Controls → f2 Custom controls (shooting) → Sub-selector center → AF-area mode + AF-ON → Auto-area AF**

This is the best complement to the other controls because it adds a broad “find the subject anywhere” option without duplicating Fn1 or the lens buttons.

| Situation | How to use the sub-selector center |
|---|---|
| Subject suddenly enters a different part of the frame | Press and hold the joystick center; Auto-area AF searches broadly and starts autofocus. |
| Bird is difficult to place inside C1 at 600mm | Use the joystick center for the initial broad acquisition. |
| Animal is isolated against sky, grass, or a clean background | Hold the joystick center and shoot while Auto-area AF and the bank’s subject detection remain reliable. |
| Several subjects are visible | Use Auto-area AF to reveal detected choices, then use the multi-selector controls when the camera offers subject switching. |
| Precise tracking is needed after acquisition | Use Fn1 for 3D-tracking + AF-ON. |
| A branch, grass, or another animal confuses Auto-area | Use a lens L-Fn button for the smaller Wide-area AF (C1) box. |

Auto-area AF uses the active bank’s subject-detection choice: Animals in A, Birds in B, People in C, and Auto only when D has temporarily been changed from MF to AF. Releasing the joystick center restores the bank’s normal AF-area mode.

Avoid assigning **AE/AF lock** here: AF-ON-only back-button focus makes that default role less valuable. Avoid using it merely to select the center focus point because the OK button can perform that job. Steve Perry’s control philosophy favors programmable AF-area overrides—especially Auto-area, 3D, Single-point, and Custom Wide—and Nikon specifically documents the sub-selector center as a temporary AF-area override when **f15 Prefer sub-selector center** is On. Nikon reference: [f15 Prefer Sub-selector Center](https://onlinemanual.nikonimglib.com/z8/en/csmf_prefer_sub-selector_center_274.html).

##### How to use the video controls

- **AF-ON** focuses at the transition speed selected under **g6 AF speed**. Use it for ordinary, controlled focus changes.
- **Sub-selector center → Fast AF-ON** ignores g6 and focuses at maximum speed. Use it when an animal or bird suddenly moves and obtaining focus matters more than a cinematic transition.
- **Fn1 → Cycle AF-area mode** changes only the area mode. After choosing an area, use AF-ON or Fast AF-ON to focus. Unlike photo mode, the Z8 firmware 3.00 g2 menu does not offer **AF-area mode + AF-ON** as a single video assignment.
- **Fn2 → Zoom on/off** magnifies the display around the focus point for confirmation. Use it before recording or when the camera is stable; it does not perform an optical zoom.
- In Bank D, **Fn2 → Focus peaking display** is more valuable than display magnification because D normally uses MF. Focus peaking must be enabled and configured under **a13 Focus peaking**.
- **Lens Fn → AF lock only** prevents the video AF system from hunting to grass, branches, or another animal after focus has been acquired with the 180–600mm.
- **BKT → Pattern tone range** cycles the zebra tone-range choice. Return it to **Highlights** for the companion’s clipping-warning workflow.
- Assigning **Record videos** to the shutter-release button allows recording without moving the right hand away from the long-lens shooting grip. The physical red-dot Video record button continues to start and stop recording as well.
- The vertical Fn, vertical multi-selector, and vertical AF-ON entries belong to the optional MB-N12 grip and can be ignored when it is not attached.

Zebra settings are located under **CUSTOM SETTINGS MENU → g Video → g15 Zebra pattern** and **g16 Limit zebra pattern tone range**. They are not in the Video Recording Menu. A highlight threshold of **245** gives an early warning near clipping; Nikon allows values from 120 to 255, with 255 showing only areas that may already be overexposed. Zebras are display aids and are not recorded in the video.

**Why Red instead of Gray:** Red is easier to see quickly against Tanzania's tan grass, brown fur, gray animals, white clouds, dust, and the flatter-looking SDR or N-Log preview. Gray can blend into both highlights and texture, particularly on pale fur and overcast skies. Red changes only the on-screen warning and is never recorded. Choose Gray instead if red is distracting or difficult to distinguish because of color vision; the pattern and threshold still work the same way.

In Bank D, focus peaking takes priority over zebras during manual focus. After confirming focus, turn **a13 Focus peaking display → Off** if you need to see the zebra pattern.

## Auto Capture

Auto Capture can automatically start a burst or video when a subject meets selected **motion, distance, subject-detection, target-area, and timing** criteria. It is useful with a stationary, tripod-mounted camera aimed at a predictable animal path, watering point, or bird perch. It is generally unnecessary for active handheld shooting from a safari vehicle.

Use adequate battery power, frame wider than usual, test that green detection boxes appear correctly, and remember that every enabled criterion must be satisfied before capture starts. Auto Capture presets are separate from the A–D bank concept.

## Switching paired banks correctly

### Photo mode

1. Choose **i Menu → Photo shooting menu bank** and select A, B, C, or D.
2. Choose **i Menu → Custom settings bank** and select the same letter.
3. Confirm image quality, focus mode, AF-area mode, subject detection, shutter speed, ISO behavior, and release mode.

### Video mode

1. Move the selector to video.
2. Choose **i Menu → Video shooting menu bank** and select the matching letter.
3. Confirm frame rate, shutter speed, file type, subject detection, microphone behavior, and electronic VR.

### Extended photo menu banks

Enable Extended Photo Menu Banks only after testing. They allow exposure mode, shutter speed, and aperture to follow the Photo bank, but they do not dynamically adjust Bank D shutter speed when the lens is zoomed. Verify the exposure after every bank or focal-length change.

## Pre-shoot verification

1. Confirm Photo A and Custom A are paired.
2. Confirm Photo B and Custom B are paired.
3. Confirm Photo C and Custom C are paired.
4. Confirm Photo D and Custom D are paired.
5. Confirm focus mode, AF-area mode, and subject-detection type in every bank.
6. Confirm shutter speed after changing subjects or focal length.
7. Confirm Auto ISO is active in A–C and manual ISO is set appropriately in D.
8. Confirm Fn1 3D-tracking + AF-ON override works.
9. Confirm Fn2 uses **Recall shooting functions** without “(hold)” in A–C.
10. Confirm every selected Fn2 submenu value and AF-ON in A–C; test press, hold, shoot, and release.
11. Confirm **f15 Prefer sub-selector center → On** and test Auto-area AF + AF-ON with a joystick-center press.
12. Confirm RAW or RAW+JPEG and the intended RAW compression in every bank.
13. Confirm VR is Off when Bank D is used on a tripod.
14. Record and review a short video in Video Banks A–D.
15. Check audio meters and listen to a test clip made with the built-in microphone.
16. Test Bank D manual focus and tripod workflow at 70mm and 180mm.
17. Confirm Custom Bank D Fn2 toggles **Starlight view** and that the live histogram disappears while Starlight view is active.
18. Confirm the complete f1 Photo and g1 Video i Menu layouts are copied to Custom Banks A–D.
19. Press **DISP** and confirm the live histogram appears on both the monitor and viewfinder in Photo and Video Custom Banks A–D.
20. Confirm adequate card capacity, charged batteries, and correct date/time.
21. Save the finished setup to card and copy the original, unrenamed **NCSET*** file to a computer.
22. Test the companion offline after updating it.
